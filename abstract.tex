\begin{abstract}
\begin{spacing}{1.5}
Underwater scene reconstruction faces dual challenges of optical degradation and dynamic motion. Existing static underwater methods cannot handle moving objects, while general dynamic reconstruction methods ignore water physical properties. This paper proposes migrating SeaSplat's underwater physical modeling to the 4DGaussians dynamic framework, building a unified reconstruction system. Main contributions include: embedding underwater imaging models (attenuation and backscatter networks) for color restoration; introducing Depth Anything V2 depth supervision for geometric consistency; designing a four-stage progressive training strategy to mitigate gradient conflicts. Experiments on Robot, Coral, Fish, and Streaks scenes achieve average PSNR of 30.54 dB, surpassing UDR-GS by 1.6\% and 4DGS by 3.1\%, with 33.29 dB on Coral, validating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
\end{spacing}
\end{abstract}
