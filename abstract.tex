随着深度学习技术在计算机视觉领域的快速发展，三维场景重建技术经历了从传统多视图几何到神经辐射场（Neural Radiance Fields, NeRF）的范式转变。三维高斯溅射（3D Gaussian Splatting, 3DGS）作为新兴的实时辐射场渲染技术，以其高效的训练速度和实时渲染能力受到广泛关注。然而，现有方法主要针对空气中的静态场景，对水下环境的特殊物理特性考虑不足，也难以处理动态场景。

该文提出将SeaSplat的水下物理建模技术迁移至4DGaussians动态场景重建框架，构建能够同时处理水下光学退化与动态场景变化的统一重建系统。主要工作包括：（1）将基于物理的水下成像模型（衰减网络、后向散射网络及相关损失函数）嵌入动态场景重建流程；（2）引入基于Depth Anything V2的单目深度监督机制，提升几何一致性；（3）设计分阶段训练策略，通过参数冻结与突发更新缓解梯度冲突。实验结果表明，该方法在Robot、Fish、Coral、Streaks四个动态水下场景上平均峰值信噪比（Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio, PSNR）达30.54 dB，较UDR-GS提升1.6\%，较4DGaussians提升3.1\%，在Coral场景达33.29 dB，验证了三组件的互补性与方法的有效性。

\begin{abstract}
\begin{spacing}{1.5}
With the rapid advancement of deep learning in computer vision, 3D scene reconstruction has evolved from traditional multi-view geometry to Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF). 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), as an emerging real-time radiance field rendering technique, has attracted considerable attention for its efficient training and real-time rendering capabilities. However, existing methods primarily target static aerial scenes and insufficiently address the unique physical properties of underwater environments, while also struggling with dynamic scenes.

This work proposes migrating SeaSplat's underwater physical modeling into the 4DGaussians dynamic scene reconstruction framework, building a unified system capable of simultaneously handling underwater optical degradation and dynamic scene changes. The main contributions include: (1) embedding a physics-based underwater imaging model (attenuation network, backscatter network, and associated loss functions) into the dynamic reconstruction pipeline; (2) introducing monocular depth supervision based on Depth Anything V2 to improve geometric consistency; (3) designing a staged training strategy that mitigates gradient conflicts through parameter freezing and burst updates. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves an average Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR) of 30.54 dB across four dynamic underwater scenes (Robot, Fish, Coral, and Streaks), surpassing UDR-GS by 1.6\% and 4DGaussians by 3.1\%, with 33.29 dB on the Coral scene, validating the complementarity of the three components and the effectiveness of the approach.

\end{spacing}
\end{abstract}
