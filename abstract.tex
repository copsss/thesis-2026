海洋覆盖地球表面积约71\%，蕴藏着丰富的科学研究与资源开发价值，水下场景的高精度三维重建与新视角合成在海洋资源勘探、水下文化遗产考古、珊瑚礁生态监测以及自主水下航行器（AUV）和遥控水下航行器（ROV）的自主导航等任务中均具有重要应用价值，是海洋科学、水下机器人与计算机视觉领域共同关注的研究方向。然而，水体的选择性吸收、悬浮颗粒的散射效应以及水流和生物运动引起的非刚性扰动，使得直接将陆地三维重建方法应用于水下场景时往往效果不佳，光学退化与动态变化的双重挑战是水下新视角合成必须解决的核心问题。

现有静态水下重建方法能够显式处理水体退化，但难以建模动态扰动；通用动态新视角合成方法虽可表示时变几何，却通常忽略水下成像物理，容易产生颜色失真和高斯漂浮伪影。针对上述问题，本文以4DGaussians为动态场景骨架，将SeaSplat中的水下图像形成模型迁移至动态高斯渲染流程，构建面向扰动水下环境的新视角合成模型。模型引入后向散射网络和直接透射衰减网络，利用渲染深度估计水体介质对颜色的影响；同时引入Depth Anything V2生成的单目逆深度先验，约束高斯体空间分布。实验在Robot、Fish、Coral和Streaks四个动态水下场景以及SeaThru-NeRF静态水下数据集上进行。动态场景实验中，本文方法在Robot、Coral和Streaks三个场景上取得最优PSNR，平均PSNR达到30.54 dB，较UDR-GS平均提升约1.6\%，较4DGS平均提升约3.1\%。在SeaThru-NeRF静态水下数据集上，本文方法较4DGS基线基本持平或提高，且在颜色恢复方面表现更佳。单目深度监督提升了几何稳定性，而水下物理模型在颜色偏移和散射明显、运动相对稳定的场景中最为有效。

}

\begin{abstract}
\begin{spacing}{1.5}
Underwater scene reconstruction faces dual challenges of optical degradation and dynamic variations. Existing static underwater reconstruction methods can explicitly model water medium degradation but struggle to represent dynamic perturbations; general dynamic novel view synthesis methods, while capable of representing time-varying geometry, typically ignore underwater imaging physics, leading to color distortion and floating Gaussian artifacts. To address these issues, this thesis adopts 4DGaussians as the dynamic scene skeleton and migrates the underwater image formation model from SeaSplat into the dynamic Gaussian rendering pipeline, constructing a novel view synthesis model for perturbed underwater environments. The model introduces a backscatter network and a direct transmission attenuation network to estimate the influence of water medium on color using rendered depth; meanwhile, monocular inverse-depth priors generated by Depth Anything V2 are incorporated to constrain the spatial distribution of Gaussians.

Experiments are conducted on four dynamic underwater scenes (Robot, Fish, Coral, and Streaks) and the static SeaThru-NeRF dataset. In dynamic scene experiments, the proposed method achieves the best PSNR on Robot, Coral, and Streaks, with an average PSNR of 30.54 dB, representing improvements of approximately 1.6\% over UDR-GS and 3.1\% over 4DGS on average. On the static SeaThru-NeRF dataset, the proposed method is comparable to or better than the 4DGS baseline and demonstrates superior color recovery. Monocular depth supervision improves geometric stability, while the underwater physical model is most effective in scenes with evident color shift and scattering and relatively stable motion.
\end{spacing}
\end{abstract}
