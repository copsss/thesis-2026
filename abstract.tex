\begin{abstract}
\begin{spacing}{1.5}
Underwater scene reconstruction faces dual challenges of optical degradation and dynamic motion. Existing static underwater methods cannot handle moving objects, while general dynamic reconstruction methods ignore water physical properties. This paper proposes migrating SeaSplat's underwater physical modeling to the 4DGaussians dynamic framework, building a unified reconstruction system. Main contributions include: embedding attenuation and backscatter networks to jointly estimate water parameters for color restoration; introducing  depth supervision for geometric consistency; designing a multi-stage progressive training strategy to mitigate gradient conflicts. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves superior rendering quality compared to existing baselines across multiple dynamic underwater scenes, validating the effectiveness and complementarity of each component.
\end{spacing}
\end{abstract}
